## Part 1: What Is HAARP?

### Official Name & Purpose

**H.A.A.R.P.** = High-frequency Active Auroral Research Program

**Official Location:** Gakona, Alaska
**Coordinates:** 62°23'30"N, 145°09'00"W
**Original Funding:** U.S. Air Force, U.S. Navy, DARPA
**Current Owner:** University of Alaska Fairbanks (since 2015)

### Technical Specifications

**Antenna Array:**
- 180 antenna towers
- 33-acre field
- Phased array configuration

**Power:**
- Effective Radiated Power (ERP): **3.6 megawatts** (officially)
- Some sources cite up to **1 gigawatt** ERP

**Frequency Range:**
- 2.8 MHz to 10 MHz (HF band)
- Primary operating frequency around **2.7-10 MHz**

### Official Purpose

**Stated Research Goals:**
1. Study ionospheric physics
2. Improve radio communication
3. Surveillance applications
4. Investigate ionospheric electron density

**How It Works:**
1. Transmits focused HF radio waves into the ionosphere
2. Temporarily excites (heats) a small portion of the ionosphere
3. Creates artificial "airglow" (aurora-like effects)
4. Studies the ionosphere's response
