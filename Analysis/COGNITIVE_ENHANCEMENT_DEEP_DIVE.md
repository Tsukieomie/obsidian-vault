## Part 2: Brain Stimulation Technologies

### Transcranial Magnetic Stimulation (TMS)

**How It Works:**
- Magnetic coil placed on skull
- Generates electromagnetic pulses
- Stimulates specific brain regions
- Can enhance or inhibit activity

**Cognitive Effects:**
| Brain Region | Enhancement |
|--------------|-------------|
| **Dorsolateral PFC** | Working memory, planning |
| **Parietal cortex** | Attention, spatial processing |
| **Motor cortex** | Skill learning |
| **Language areas** | Verbal fluency |

**Military Research:**
- DARPA funded TMS studies
- Tested on soldiers for:
  - Accelerated skill acquisition
  - Enhanced marksmanship
  - Improved vigilance

### Transcranial Direct Current Stimulation (tDCS)

**How It Works:**
- Low electrical current (1-2 mA)
- Delivered through electrodes on scalp
- Modulates neuronal activity
- Can be done at home with simple device

**Documented Enhancements:**
| Application | Effect |
|-------------|--------|
| **Learning** | 2x faster skill acquisition |
| **Memory** | Improved recall |
| **Attention** | Extended vigilance |
| **Decision-making** | Better under stress |

**Air Force Research:**
> Studies showed tDCS could double the speed at which drone operators learned target detection.
