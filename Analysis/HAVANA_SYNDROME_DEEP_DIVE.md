## Part 1: The Moscow Signal (1953-1979) - Historical Precedent

### Official Source
**National Security Archive, George Washington University:** https://nsarchive.gwu.edu/briefing-book/intelligence-russia-programs/2022-09-13/moscow-signals-declassified-microwave

### What Happened

**Timeline:**
- **1953:** U.S. technicians first detected energy rays directed at U.S. Embassy in Moscow
- **Early 1960s:** Active monitoring began
- **1964:** Numerous bugging devices discovered in Embassy walls
- **Mid-1975:** Signals continued; additional and stronger signals detected
- **1976:** Public scandal triggered congressional hearings
- **1979:** Senate investigation concluded

**Technical Details:**
- Soviet intelligence directed low-level electromagnetic beams (microwaves) at the U.S. Embassy daily
- Beam was 50 feet across
- Power density: "no greater than .05 mw/cm" (below U.S. and Soviet safety levels)
- Late 1950s: Ionizing radiation also penetrated the U.S. ambassador's residence

### Official U.S. Investigations

| Project | Agency | Years | Cost | Purpose |
|---------|--------|-------|------|---------|
| **TUMS** | White House | 1965+ | - | Code name for Moscow Signal investigation |
| **PANDORA** | ARPA (now DARPA) | 1965-1970 | $4,615,000 | Determine threat of Moscow Signal |
| **BIZARRE** | ARPA | 1966-1969 | - | Radiation experiments on monkeys |
| **Big Boy** | ARPA | - | - | Medical evaluations of USS Saratoga crew |

### Key Findings

**Monkey Experiments (Project BIZARRE):**
- Initial experiment (with inaccurate higher power readings): Appeared to show behavioral impact
- Subsequent experiments (corrected power levels): "No overt primate performance degradation"
- RAND Corporation review: "No material...which is scientifically credible" of any impact
- Criticized for "poor" animal care (3 monkey deaths from strangulation due to experimental design)

**Human Studies:**
- Blood samples taken from U.S. personnel under false pretenses ("monitoring Russian viruses")
- USS Saratoga study: "No discernible evidence of physiological and psychological differences"

**1979 Senate Committee Conclusion:**
> "No convincing evidence was discovered that could directly implicate the exposure to microwave radiation experienced by the employees at the Moscow Embassy in the causation of any adverse health effects"

**However, the Committee cautioned:**
> "It is too early to have been able to detect long-term mortality effects"

### Probable Purpose of Moscow Signal
1. Jamming device to disrupt U.S. espionage operations
2. **Most likely:** Activate, power, and/or interpret eavesdropping devices implanted in Embassy walls
3. Initially suspected (but disproved): Degrade abilities of U.S. personnel

### Connection to Havana Syndrome

Former diplomat James Schumacher wrote:
> "It is like 'déjà vu' all over again."

Former *Foreign Policy* editor Sharon Weinberger noted the alleged use of a "sound weapon" in Cuba:
> "Harks back to a Cold War medical mystery"

**Status:** Moscow Signal records are being reviewed by Biden administration panel investigating Havana Syndrome
