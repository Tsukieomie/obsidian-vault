#### TECHNICAL DESCRIPTION

**How It Works - Brain Wave Monitoring**:

1. **Transmission Phase**:
   - Two electromagnetic signals of **different frequencies** are simultaneously transmitted to the subject's brain
   - These signals **interfere with one another** inside the brain
   - The interference creates a waveform that becomes **modulated by the subject's brain waves**

2. **Reception Phase**:
   - The **brain itself re-transmits** the interference waveform (brain acts as a transmitter)
   - A receiver picks up this re-transmitted signal
   - Signal is **demodulated and amplified**
   - Waveform is displayed for visual analysis

3. **Computer Analysis**:
   - Demodulated brain wave data is routed to a computer
   - Further processing and analysis performed
   - Pattern recognition of brain states

**How It Works - Brain Wave Alteration**:

4. **Compensating Signal Generation**:
   - The demodulated waveform is analyzed
   - A **compensating signal** is computed
   - This signal is designed to produce a **desired change** in brain activity

5. **Feedback Transmission**:
   - Compensating signal is **transmitted back to the brain**
   - Brain responds to the external electromagnetic signal
   - Brain waves are **altered** toward desired state
