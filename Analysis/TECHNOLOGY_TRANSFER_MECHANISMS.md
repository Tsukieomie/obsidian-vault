## STEP 3: Patent Filing - Claiming Ownership

### Who Files Patents?

**University Technology Transfer Offices:**
- Stanford Office of Technology Licensing
- MIT Technology Licensing Office
- UC Berkeley's Office of Intellectual Property
- Etc.

**Process:**
1. Researcher notifies tech transfer office of invention
2. Office evaluates commercial potential
3. Patent attorney files application with USPTO
4. University listed as assignee (owns the patent)
5. Researcher listed as inventor (credit, potential royalties)

### Example: Optogenetics Patent

**US Patent 7,883,536**
- **Title:** Method for optical stimulation of neurons
- **Assignee:** The Board of Trustees of the Leland Stanford Junior University
- **Inventors:** Karl Deisseroth, Feng Zhang, et al.
- **Filed:** January 11, 2007
- **Granted:** February 8, 2011
- **Funded By:** DARPA (acknowledged in patent)

**Significance:**
- DARPA funded the research
- Stanford owns the patent
- Technology available for licensing
- Cited by 500+ other patents (foundation tech)

### Patent Strategy

**Universities File Broad Claims:**
- Cover all possible applications (medical, military, commercial)
- Make it hard to design around
- Maximize licensing value

**Example - Neural Dust Patent (UC Berkeley):**
Covers any microscopic wireless sensor powered by ultrasound for biological monitoring. Doesn't matter if you use it for medical, research, or surveillance - patent covers ALL uses.

**This is How They Get You:**
- Military application = surveillance
- But patent = "medical monitoring"
- Deploy for surveillance, claim medical use

## STEP 4: Technology Transfer Act - The Legal Loophole

### National Technology Transfer and Advancement Act of 1995

**Official Purpose:** "Share costs of R&D with private sector"

**Real Effect:** Enables military research to become consumer products with zero oversight

### Key Provisions

**15 USC §3710:**
> "Each Federal agency shall... establish goals for technology transfer"

**Translation:** Government MUST give taxpayer-funded research to corporations

**15 USC §3710a:**
> "Federal laboratories may enter into cooperative research agreements with private companies"

**Translation:** Direct pipeline from lab to company

**No Requirement For:**
- Public notice
- Safety review
- Ethics oversight
- Consent from public
- Disclosure of military origins

**Result:** DARPA weapon → Consumer product, legally

### How It Works in Practice

**Example: Neural Monitoring Tech**

```
DARPA funds Stanford (optogenetics research)
    ↓
Stanford files patent (owns IP)
    ↓
Technology Transfer Act allows:
    ↓
Stanford licenses patent to Corporation X
    ↓
Corporation X develops consumer product
    ↓
Public buys product (unaware of DARPA origin)
```

**Everybody Wins (Except the Public):**
- DARPA: Gets weapon deployed without doing it directly
- University: Gets licensing revenue
- Corporation: Gets cutting-edge tech
- Public: Gets surveilled without knowing

## STEP 5: Corporate Licensing - The Money Changes Hands

### How Universities License Patents

**Two Models:**

**A. Exclusive License**
- One company gets all rights
- University gets:
  - Upfront payment ($100K-$millions)
  - Royalties (3-10% of product revenue)
  - Milestone payments
- Company gets: Exclusive right to commercialize

**B. Non-Exclusive License**
- Multiple companies can use tech
- Lower fees per licensee
- Broader deployment

### Who Gets the Licenses?

**Typical Licensees for Neurotechnology:**
1. **Medical Device Companies** (cover story)
   - Medtronic, Boston Scientific, Abbott
   - Justification: "Help patients"

2. **Tech Giants** (real deployers)
   - [[Entities/Apple|Apple]], [[Entities/Google|Google]], Microsoft
   - Often via acquisition of startups (see below)

3. **Defense Contractors** (military use)
   - [[Entities/Battelle_Memorial_Institute|Battelle]], Raytheon, Lockheed Martin
   - Direct military application

4. **Startups** (intermediaries)
   - Faculty-founded companies
   - Later acquired by Big Tech
   - Example: [[Entities/FreerLogic|FreerLogic]] (likely licensed or developed similar tech)

### Example: How Apple Got Neural Monitoring

**Hypothesized Path (based on patent US 10,945,618 filed 2018):**

**Option 1: License from University**
- Apple licensed EEG sensor technology from university
- Adapted for wrist-based detection
- Filed own patent (improvement on base tech)

**Option 2: Hire Researchers**

**Option 3: Acquire Startup**

**FOIA Requests:**
1. "All DARPA grants to [University] for neurotechnology (2010-2025)"
2. "All technology licensing agreements between [University] and [Corporation]"

- Check "Government Interest" section (discloses federal funding)
- Track citations (who built on this patent)
- Forward citations (where did technology go)

**NIH RePORTER:**
- Database of all NIH grants: projectreporter.nih.gov
- Search by institution, researcher, keyword
- See who got money for what

**NSF Awards Database:**
- Similar to NIH RePORTER
- Search NSF grants to universities and companies

**Academic Publications:**
- Papers acknowledge funding sources
- Cross-reference DARPA/NIH/NSF funding
