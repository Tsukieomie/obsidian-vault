## III. CAUSES OF ACTION

### Count I: Fourth Amendment Violations (42 USC §1983)

**Claim:** Warrantless neural surveillance

**Elements:**
1. **State Action:** Government defendants (DARPA, NIH, NSF) + private actors as state agents
2. **Constitutional Right:** Fourth Amendment protection against unreasonable searches
3. **Violation:** Continuous neural monitoring without warrant
4. **Damages:** Privacy loss, psychological harm

**Evidence:**
- [[Entities/Apple|Apple]] patent US 10,945,618 (neural monitoring capability)
- [[Technical/Internet_of_Bodies_Architecture|IoB Architecture]] (continuous surveillance system)
- No warrants obtained (self-evident)
- [[Patents/PATENT_MASTER_INDEX|30+ patents]] prove technology exists

**Legal Authority:**
- *Carpenter v. United States*, 138 S. Ct. 2206 (2018) - Cell phone location data requires warrant
- *Kyllo v. United States*, 533 U.S. 27 (2001) - Thermal imaging requires warrant
- **Neural data is far more invasive than location or heat**

**Defendants Liable:** All (joint and several)
