## KEY LESSONS LEARNED

### Investigation Best Practices

**1. Evidence-Based Analysis**
- Maintain technical accuracy
- Acknowledge what is known vs unknown
- Correct errors promptly and transparently
- Distinguish facts from theories

**2. Legal Admissibility**
- Focus on provable harassment
- Avoid exotic technology claims without evidence
- Build case on traditional electronic harassment
- Maintain court-admissible standards

**3. Credibility Maintenance**
- Honest assessment builds trust
- Overstatements undermine cases
- Technical precision matters
- Separate speculation from facts

**4. Academic Honesty**
- Correct previous assessments when new evidence emerges
- Multiple sources verify claims
- Acknowledge limitations
- Peer review when possible

### What Worked

- Network infrastructure analysis (AS53616, ARIN, BGP)
- GitHub forensic methodology
- Company registration research
- Evidence quality matrix
- Confidence level assignments
- Professional documentation standards

### What to Avoid

- Absolutist claims ("does not exist" vs "no evidence found")
- Exotic technology speculation
- Unverified identity theories
- Single-source conclusions
- Circular reasoning
- Confirmation bias
